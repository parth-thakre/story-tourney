# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo developers and AI/LLM enthusiasts running story tournaments locally. They open the tool,
paste a prompt, hit run, then watch four models compete. They are curious about LLM capability
differences and drawn to the suspense of the reveal. They use it alone, at a desk, often at
night. The session is self-contained: one tournament, one sitting.

## Product Purpose

Story Tournament orchestrates a blind literary competition between four AI models. One shared
prompt, four stories, blind peer review, revision, final ranking, and a ceremonial reveal of
who wrote what. The product exists to make LLM comparison feel like an event rather than a
benchmark. Success is the moment the winner card unfolds and the author name appears.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, ceremonial, understated. The tone is that of a literary journal that takes its
subject seriously but never shouts. Quiet confidence. The interface steps back so the stories
step forward.

## Anti-references

- SaaS dashboards: sidebar nav, metric cards, data tables, activity feeds, badge counts.
- AI chatbot UI: bubble threads, ephemeral feel, bottom input bar.
- LLM benchmark sites (LMSYS/Chatbot Arena): spreadsheets, radar charts, scientific framing.
- Neon futurism: cyberpunk glows, magenta on black.

## Design Principles

1. **The story is the product.** Interface chrome stays out of the way. Typography, spacing, and
   color exist to surface narrative, not to demonstrate visual sophistication.
2. **Earn the reveal.** The winner announcement is the emotional peak. Every prior screen builds
   toward it. Pacing matters.
3. **Suspense through restraint.** Withhold information until it is time to show it. Blind labels
   during the run, author names only at reveal. Design mirrors this discipline.
4. **One idea per section.** No stacked cards inside stacked cards. No sidebar navigation.
   Three screens, each with a single job.
5. **Dark by scene.** The interface is dark because this is a solitary, late-night, focused
   activity. Not dark because tools look cool dark.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA contrast throughout.
- Reduced motion: all entrance animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- No color-only status signaling: phase states use shape + text alongside color.
